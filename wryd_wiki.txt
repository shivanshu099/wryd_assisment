Act 1 : Our Past
You know how they say every great story starts with someone having a brilliant idea? Yeah, this isn't that story.
You ever look back at your life choices and think, "Damn, that was either really brave or really stupid"? That's basically our origin story.
CHAPTER 1: Audacity: A Love Story between Dreams & Delusion
"We had the kind of education that teaches you mitochondria is the powerhouse of the cell but leaves you clueless about taxes, filmmaking, or the art of professional bullshitting. But hey, who needs experience when you have audacity, right?  And because nobody told us we couldn't. Or maybe they did, and we weren't listening. You know that moment in horror movies where everyone's screaming "Don't go in there!" at the screen? We were the idiots running straight into the haunted house of filmmaking, armed with nothing but audacity and a borrowed camera. Basically, the kind of confidence you can only get from having absolutely no idea what you're doing.
I know what you're thinking – "How bad could it be?"
See, most people graduate high school and think about college. We graduated and thought, "Hey, you know what sounds fun? Let's make films!" With no equipment. No connections. And definitely no clue. The kind of decision that makes your parents question their genetic contribution to your intelligence.
CHAPTER 2: A Symphony of Spectacular Fuck-ups (Our greatest hits album, featuring zero hits)
We kept failing up. Like, aggressively up. The kind of failing where you're not sure if you're getting better or just getting better at hiding the mistakes. You know what's funnier than failing? Failing with an audience. Every weekend we'd tell our parents "This is it!" By Monday, we were experts in creative excuses. "The sun wasn't artistic enough" was actually used once.  We had more "final attempts" than a toxic relationship has "last chances."  We had so many failed attempts at IFP's 50-hour challenge, we could've made a film about not making films. Spoiler: We probably would've failed at that too.
Friends came and went – some couldn't handle the chaos, others wanted safer paths. We were learning the hardest lesson in creativity: not everyone who starts the journey makes it to the wyrd part.  Turns out, "Trust me, this will work" isn't as convincing the seventh time around. But it did work.
CHAPTER 3: The Universe Blinked First (Turns out stubbornness is a business strategy)
"All The Best" happened.  Not because we finally got good – oh no, that would be too logical. We developed immunity to embarrassment. We just failed so many times we ran out of fucks to give about doing it "right." Two survivors of our own amateur hour, making something that didn't completely suck. 5,000 YouTube views. To put that in perspective, that's about 4,990 more views than our moms could generate with fake accounts.
CHAPTER 4: When the Ball Started Rolling (And We Couldn’t Stop It)
Then came what I like to call our "Yes, And?" era. You know how they say desperation is the mother of invention? Well, we were like a whole orphanage of invention. "Can you meme?" Sure! "Design websites?" Why not! "Build a rocket to Mars?" Let me watch a YouTube tutorial real quick.  We were basically a startup inside a film school inside a creative agency. We were taking up any and every creative gig that could fund our film addiction.
Then came Movifi's 50-hour challenge. Now, this is where it gets good. We're in this village that Google Maps probably lists as "Loading..." . We’re running on cups of chai and poor life choices, probably hallucinating from sleep deprivation. You know how some people say constraints breed creativity? Try making a film in 50 hours with a crew running on dreams and deadline panic. But here's the thing – sometimes when you don't have time to doubt yourself, magic happens.
We win Best Film and Best Director. The jury must've been as sleep-deprived as we were. Or maybe – just maybe – being too tired to overthink actually makes you better at art.
"Kakbagodaa"  - Our little film that could, did. We travelled with the film from the biggest cities to the remotest village in multiple film festivals. Some friends who'd jumped ship earlier? They saw what we were building and swam back.
Then Covid hit. Everyone's making banana bread like it's gonna save humanity. Meanwhile, what are WE doing? Oh, just casually MAKING FILMS IN A PANDEMIC. If that sounds insane, that's because it absolutely fucking was. While people are learning to unmute on Zoom, we're out here with cameras like "Yeah, this seems like the perfect time to double down on bad decisions!"  because apparently, our survival instinct was as broken as our work-life balance.
And here's the beautiful part - you know what happens? After years of making IFP our personal highlight reel of failures - after so many attempts they should've named a rejection letter after us - we finally WIN. With "Chalte Rahiye Janaab." During a PANDEMIC.
We had to prove it wasn't a fluke. Made "Jama" and "Peekaboo" back-to-back after it.
CHAPTER 5: The Bombae Chronicles
Akarsh Khurana (yes, @akvarious) spotted us at a festival. Ghosted us and then pulled a reverse Uno card and came back with "Jugaadistan" for Lionsgate Play. Mumbai came calling. That city that eats dreams and shits reality shows.
Now, most people would take this as their big break. We took it as permission to be sleep-deprived in a more expensive city.  Our first big set experience. Living the dream! You know, that dream where you're constantly exhausted but somehow still functioning? That one. Meanwhile, we're still winning at IFP with “Stand Out” in 2021 &  "Kachcha Papitaa" in 2022 because apparently, we also needed to test if we could be in two places at once.
CHAPTER 6: Delhi Round 2: It’s Personal this time! (How to steal your future back)
After running around with Lionsgate, Netflix, Zee5 - living that sweet studio life - we head back to Delhi. And here's the gut punch - some of the crew comes back with us, rides The Urban Desi wave 2.0, then bounces. It stung. Like watching your kids leave for college, except you're barely older than college age yourself and your kids are actually your friends who you've emotionally adopted.
CHAPTER 7: Turns Out We Were Right For All The Wrong Reasons (Spoiler: Maybe we did all along)
Here’s the wyrd thing - They say every great story starts with someone crazy enough to try something that shouldn't work. Well, this is a story about being crazy enough to keep trying even after proving multiple times that it definitely doesn't work. And here's the thing about banging your head against a wall - sometimes the wall gives up before you do. And when it breaks – it's worth every sleepless night, every failed attempt, every friend who thought we were insane.
Because in the end, we didn't just make films. We made proof that sometimes, the best talent you can have is the inability to take no for an answer. Even when that 'no' is coming from reality itself. We convinced an entire industry we knew what we were doing. And somewhere between our third IFP win and people actually returning our calls, we realized - holy shit, maybe we do! Some of the best people you'll ever meet are the ones crazy enough to believe in your bullshit.
People ask me if we'd do it differently. Hell NO!
Because here's the thing about being stupid enough to chase your dreams – sometimes you catch them, sometimes they catch you, but the chase? That's where the story is.
And if there's one thing we're good at, it's making one hell of a story.
That's The Urban Desi story. Not 13 short films, festival wins, some adverts and fancy studio projects - though yeah, we did that too. It's the story of failing upwards so spectacularly that success finally went "Damn, I need to get me some of that. Every success story reaches a point where evolution isn't just an option—it's an imperative. While TUD had carved its niche in independent filmmaking, the industry was calling for something more comprehensive, more ambitious, more wyrdr.

Enter Wyrd Media Labs.

Act 2: The Wyrd Dawn
The Urban Desi was about learning how to tell stories. Breaking rules. Making shit happen with nothing but audacity. Here's what happens when you spend years making films: You start seeing stories everywhere. 
You ever have one of those moments where the universe just gets tired of being subtle and slaps you with symbolism?  So there's our founder, sitting at this tapri in Old Delhi, having one of those existential crisis moments. You know the kind - where you're staring into your cutting chai like it's got all the answers to 'what the hell am I doing with my life?’ And then this rickshaw wallah cruises by wearing a Web of Wyrd t-shirt. Not even in a cool way – just a basic black tee with the Web of Wyrd on it. And that's when the brain went: "Wait. What if we've been asking all the wrong questions?” 
Speaking of AI - Here's the thing about AI that nobody's talking about - it's actually pretty stupid at being creative. Like, it's great at pretending to be creative. It can write you a thousand captions that sound exactly like every other caption ever written. But original thinking? That's still our thing. You know what AI is really good at though? All the boring shit nobody wants to do anyway. The endless 'data-driven insights' that somehow always lead to the same boring content.
You know what's really artificial? The whole system. Everyone thinks you need a Bombay address to have a Bombay dream. Like creativity follows pin codes or something. It's like telling a painter they can only make art if they live next to an art gallery. 
India's creative industry has this unwritten geography book:
Bombay: Where creativity is officially allowed to exist
Delhi: Where creativity goes to become a government job
Bangalore: Where creativity needs a JavaScript framework
Everyone else: "Beta, focus on real career"
You spend enough time in this industry, you start seeing patterns. Everyone's got a process. A framework. A system. People turning art into algebra. Making content like they're following a recipe fora bland dish - 'Add two parts trend analysis, one part best practices, remove all traces of personality, serve lukewarm.'
Think about it - everyone's trying to make AI more human. Teaching it to tell jokes, write poetry, make art. Meanwhile, humans are out there trying to be more like AI. Following formulas. Optimizing engagement. A/B testing their personality out of existence. It's like we're all running in opposite directions but ending up in the same boring place.
But that rickshaw wallah with the Web of Wyrd shirt? That was our moment. Because the Web of Wyrd isn't just some cool Norse design that accidentally ended up on a t-shirt in Old Delhi. It's this ancient symbol about how everything's connected - past, present, future, all weaving together in ways that make no logical sense but somehow work anyway. And if that's not a sign, we don't know what is.
So yeah, maybe it took a random t-shirt in Old Delhi to crystallize this vision. But isn't that how all great wyrd tales begin? With something so improbable it makes coincidence look like careful planning?
Remember: Logic didn't build the pyramids. Logic didn't put a man on the moon. Logic definitely didn't create The Urban Desi. And logic sure as hell isn't going to build what comes next.
You know how they say evolution is a slow process? They lied. Sometimes it happens in an instant.
So we built Wyrd Media Labs.
Wyrd isn't TUD 2.0. It's not an upgrade or a pivot or whatever bullshit term consultants use when they can't explain what's happening. Wyrd is what happens when stories get tired of being just stories. When technology gets bored of being just tools. When reality itself looks at what you're doing and goes "Fuck it, I'm in.”
Everyone's out here trying to predict the future. Drawing trend lines. Making forecasts. Meanwhile, we're sitting here going "What if we just... made our own?" Not in some dystopian tech bro way. More like that kid who wasn't satisfied with any toy in the store so they built their own from scratch. Except instead of toys, we're building cool stuff.
Here's the beautiful part about where we are now: We don't know what's next. And for the first time, that's exactly where we want to be. Because Wyrd isn't about knowing the destination – it's about building new paths. Ones that don't exist on any map. 
And if anyone asks what's next? Well, that's like asking what's next while you're in the middle of inventing electricity. You don't know. That's the whole point. You just know nothing's going to be the same after.
Every project is a new experiment. Every idea is a potential universe. And failure? Failure just means we found another way reality bends before it breaks.
Not because we had it all figured out - hell, we're probably more confused than everyone else. But at least we're confused in an interesting way. 
P.S. That guy with the Wyrd symbol? Never saw him again. Probably went back to whatever dimension sends cosmic signs to people drinking chai in Old Delhi. If you're reading this, cosmic sign guy, thanks for the existential crisis. It worked out pretty wyrd.

The Inconvenient Truth About Creative Industry
      The Death Certificate of "Business As Usual"
According to Gartner's 2023 CMO Spend Survey, 72% of marketing leaders are disappointed with their agency partnerships. Yet they keep throwing money at the problem – $20.6 billion in 2023 alone. Why? Because they're stuck in an abusive relationship with mediocrity.
Meanwhile, Forrester reports that 67% of brands are building in-house teams while simultaneously increasing external creative budgets. Sounds contradictory? That's because they're not looking for agencies anymore. They're looking for something else. Something that doesn't exist yet.
The Six Sins of Modern Marketing 
1. The Feel-Good Factory
Remember when agencies were about results, not reassurance? Now they're expensive therapists for brands with identity crises. According to McKinsey, 81% of agency-client relationships are maintained through "relationship satisfaction" rather than actual performance metrics.
2. The Jack-of-All-Trades Joke
Everyone's promising everything. Full-service this, end-to-end that. Yet Deloitte's Agency Future Report shows that 89% of successful specialist agencies outperform their full-service counterparts in both revenue and client satisfaction. The secret? They're not lying about what they can't do.
3. The Attention Myth
"Shrinking attention spans" is the lie we tell ourselves to justify bad content. Harvard Business Review's latest media consumption study shows that Gen Z spends an average of 7.2 hours on a single piece of content they care about – but less than 8 seconds deciding if they care.
4. The Personalization Plague
We've personalized ourselves into a corner. Adobe's Consumer Experience Index shows that 71% of consumers find hyper-personalized content "creepy" rather than helpful. Mass uniqueness – standing for something specific that attracts your tribe – outperforms personalization by 3.4x.
5. The Underground Revolution
While everyone's obsessing over follower counts, the real influence economy has gone dark. Signal's 2023 Messaging Trends Report reveals that 84% of brand advocacy happens in private channels – WhatsApp groups, private Discord servers, closed communities. The public feed is just performance art.
6. The Agency Autopsy
The traditional agency model isn't just dying; it's decomposing. Yet something new is emerging from the compost. SignalFire's Creator Economy Report shows a 312% increase in hybrid creative-tech companies that don't fit any traditional category.
The Wyrd Way Forward
So what are we building? Not an agency. Not a production house. Not a tech startup. We're building an ecosystem where:
We're Not Your Therapist
We'll tell you what you need to hear, not what you want to hear
Your comfort zone is not our responsibility
Results over reassurance
But does this mean we won’t work towards client satisfaction? Absolutely yes. 
Instead let’s focus on fostering a human relationship with our clients. Let’s take them out for coffee, show them the beauty of Delhi. Just don’t get too chummy please. 
Expertise Has Edges
We're not full-service; we're full-send in what we do best
Collaboration over competition
Build deep, not wide
Saying no is tough. But we must always remember our roots. Having the capabilities to do everything related to media comes with a lot of pressure. 
Attention Is Earned
Stop interrupting what people love; become what people love
Make it worth their time, or don't make it at all
Quality over quantity, always
Mass Uniqueness Over Mass Personalization
Be so specific that the wrong people ignore you
Build for believers, not everyone
Stand for something or fall for anything
Dark Social First
Build for screenshot-ability
Design for DMs
Create for conversation, not just consumption
Category of One
Don't fit in; stand out
Create problems for old solutions
Make the competition irrelevant
The Bottom Line
The industry is playing checkers while pretending it's chess. We're building a new game entirely. One where creativity isn't a service but a force multiplier. Where technology isn't a feature but a fundamental. Where results aren't measured in slides but in impact.
We're not here to disrupt the industry. We're here to build what comes next.
Want to join us in the deep end? We've got cookies. They're wyrd-flavored, but that's kind of the point.
Note: All data points sourced from publicly available industry reports (2023-2024). But honestly, if you need statistics to tell you the industry is broken, you're probably part of the problem.

How we speak
Most brand voices sound like they were created by AI pretending to be human pretending to be professional pretending to be casual. It's like inception, but with more buzzwords.
Everyone's got a brand voice now. Like how everyone's got anxiety and a podcast - except the anxiety is probably more authentic.
"We're human-centric." As opposed to what? Dolphin-centric? Penguin-centric? (Although we'd actually read a penguin-centric marketing strategy)
“We're disruptive!" Yeah, like a toddler at a funeral - technically true, completely missing the point.
After deep introspection and hundreds of ridiculous conversations, we identified five core elements that make our voice distinctly Wyrd.
The Character Profile 
These are our five elements. Think of them as the five horsemen of actually saying something real - except instead of bringing the apocalypse, they just bring the death of corporate bullshit.


 SOUL
Noun /sōl/
That thing your ex says you don't have. What your Spotify playlist claims to have and your screen time suggests otherwise. The intangible force that makes sunsets prettier, heartbreaks poetic, and binge-watching feel like self-care. It’s not loud, but when it speaks, it sings—or screams—and you’d better listen.


Space/Ether
It’s invisible force that connects everything, just like soul is that intangible thing that makes communication resonate. 
The space where meaning happens.

When to use it: Always, but especially when talking about why we do what we do
How to check: If it doesn't make you feel something, it's not done
 TRUTH
Noun /tro͞oTH/ 
The thing everyone swears they want until it actually shows up. Heavy, blunt, and inconvenient. It stings, and if you ignore it, it just gets louder. Not the kind that hurts feelings - the kind that changes minds. It doesn’t care about your feelings, but somehow, it always sets you free. The uncomfortable reality check in a world of comfortable lies.


Fire
Burns through bullshit, transforms what it touches, can either illuminate or destroy - just like truth. Powerful, cannot be contained, and changes everything it touches.

When to use it: When the elephant in the room needs a microphone.
How to check: Does it make you uncomfortable? Good.
 NERVE 
Noun /nûrv/
That moment when you realize the crazy idea is actually the right idea, but now you have to convince everyone else.
The audacity to leap, the courage to fail, and the boldness to try again (sometimes louder). Often mistaken for courage; always the reason for great stories. The reckless audacity to bet on yourself when everyone else is hedging their bets. It’s saying, “Let’s try it” when logic is screaming, “Maybe don’t.” What your parents think you lost when you decided to “follow your passion.” Not just guts, but guts with a game plan.


Air
Nerve is the force that moves things forward, can be a gentle breeze or a storm, invisible but powerful. Like nerve, it's about movement and pushing boundaries.

When to use it: When conventional wisdom says "play it safe"
How to check: Does this make you slightly nervous to send?
 WIT
Noun /wit/
The verbal sucker punch you wish you’d landed during that argument three months ago. The perfect comeback, the sly observation, the intellectual wink. If humor is a smile, wit is the smirk that gets there first. Intelligence having a good time. The art of being smart without being smug about it.  It’s the perfect cocktail of brainpower and sass, shaken, not stirred. Not just clever comebacks – strategic disruption disguised as entertainment.


Water
Fluid, adaptable, can cut through stone over time or crash like a wave. Like wit, it finds its way through any crack and takes the shape it needs to while maintaining its essence.

When to use it: When you need to cut through noise or make a point stick
How to check: Would this make someone smile while thinking?
 CULTURE


Earth
The ground we stand on, our roots, what gives everything else context and foundation. Like culture, it's what everything else grows from.
OUTSIDE (The Indian Culture)
Noun /kul-chər/
That thing your NRI cousin keeps trying to explain to their foreign friends. Deep roots in the streets where creativity doesn't ask for permission.  The streets where every truck is a poet. This isn't about being Indian for the sake of it – it's about tapping into a centuries-old tradition of telling stories that make you think, laugh, and feel seen, all while honking musically through traffic.


When to use it: When connecting our work to the larger world
How to check: Does this feel authentically rooted yet universally relatable?


INSIDE (The Wyrd Culture)
Noun /kul-chər/
Culture is what happens when you put smart, weird people in a room and let them be smart and weird. It's the inside jokes that make no sense to outsiders but perfect sense to us. The stuff that makes us us. Not forced fun Friday bullshit. Not "we're like family" manipulation. Culture isn’t curated; it’s chaotic, loud, and unapologetically specific.
 The Rules of Writing/Speaking/Being Wyrd
THE ONE RULE THAT RULES THEM ALL
Say what you mean. Mean what you say™


Keep It Real
Skip the fake cool
Say the obvious thing
Don't try to sound smart


Write Like a Human
No one says "leverage" in real life
If it sounds like it could've been written by ChatGPT (even if it is generated by it), delete it and try again
Read it out loud. If you cringe, rewrite it
When in doubt, ask: "Would this make someone smile while rolling their eyes?"
Talk TO people, not AT them
Use humor when it fits (it usually fits)
Share the weird thoughts - they're usually the best ones
Don't hide behind big words or corporate speak.
Write like you talk (but better)
If it sounds like corporate BS, delete it
No fancy words when simple ones work


Structure (Yes, Chaos Needs Structure)
Short paragraphs. Like this.
Mix sentence lengths. Some should punch. Others should flow like a conversation you're having with someone who's equally passionate about the topic and might occasionally forget to breathe while making their point.
Use parentheses (they're like footnotes for people with ADHD)
Em dashes are your friends — use them too enunciate.


Make It Sharp
Cut the fluff
Make your point
Move on
But make it stick


Technical Stuff That Matters

Capitalize for Emphasis When It Matters™

 See (what others miss)
 The Obvious
THE BEST IDEAS SOUND INSANE. Until they don't. Then they sound obvious.
Safe ideas die alone. Usually in meetings about meetings. Somewhere between "let's circle back" and "touching base.”
Fancy words are where weak ideas hide. Strip it naked, make it real, and let them shine.
The world needs another synergy presentation like it needs another LinkedIn influencer.
The right answer isn't always the wyrd one. Make them feel, make them think, make them remember.
Algorithms don't cry at movies or drunk-text their ex. They don’t feel, they don’t buy. Stop making them your audience.
 The Hidden
The world isn't suffering from lack of content. It's suffering from creative constipation.
Data tells you what worked yesterday. Gut tells you what works tomorrow. We trust both, but gut gets the corner office.
STORY ABOVE ALL: Metrics matter, but wyrd works wonders. If your analytics tell the whole story, you're writing the wrong book.
Want engagement? Make them feel something- their spreadsheets can't measure.
Good is the enemy of unforgettable. We aim for the latter.
 The Future


AI isn't the enemy; boring is. While everyone's afraid of AI taking their jobs, we're giving AI the jobs nobody wanted anyway. 
Robots are great at everything nobody remembers. Be great at everything nobody forgets. Let the machines optimize. You humanize.
While everyone's teaching AI to think like humans, we encourage humans to think like humans again.
AI can copy anything except your wyrd. That's your superpower.
 Do (what others won’t)
 The Wild
The best work leaves you unsure whether to frame it or hide it. Your wyrdest thought? Probably your best.
Obsession looks like madness until it works. Then they call it genius.
Make work that would make your future self proud. And slightly concerned. 
DISCLAIMER: Creation without chaos is just admin and chaos without craft is just noise. But chaos with a plan? That’s what works.
 The Mistakes
Fear is just excitement forgetting to breathe. Remind it. If you're not failing wyrdly, you're probably not trying anything interesting.
Every "wow" moment started as "wtf?”  Every breakthrough started as someone's "wait, that's stupid... unless?”. If you're not slightly embarrassed by some of your attempts, you're not trying hard enough.

If it ain't a hell yeah, it's a no.
Sure, we color outside the lines – but we keep our crayons sharp.

Make every pixel, word, frame, and moment count. If it doesn’t matter, it doesn’t belong.
Life's too short for burnout: Even your laptop knows when to sleep.
Find your groove - even if its offbeat.
